Visualization – Star Counts by Spectral Type (Altair)

This bar chart shows the distribution of stars across different spectral types, revealing that most stars in the dataset belong to the cooler F, G, and K classes, while very hot O and B type stars are comparatively rare. This pattern reflects the natural abundance of stellar types in the observable universe, where massive, hot stars are short-lived and therefore less common. The bar chart design is effective because it allows for direct comparison between discrete spectral categories, making differences in frequency immediately clear. The use of color reinforces the categorical nature of the data, and the interactive legend allows users to isolate and highlight specific spectral types, improving focus and interpretability without cluttering the visualization.

Visualization 2 – D3

This scatter plot shows the relationship between a star’s color index (B-V), which serves as a proxy for temperature, and its apparent brightness (V magnitude). The visualization reveals that cooler, redder stars (higher B-V values) tend to appear dimmer, while hotter, bluer stars (lower B-V values) are generally brighter, reflecting a fundamental pattern in stellar astronomy. The scatter plot design is effective because it clearly displays this continuous relationship between two quantitative variables, while the use of transparency helps reduce overplotting in dense regions. Interactive tooltips enhance the design by allowing users to explore individual stars in more detail without cluttering the chart.
